### Final Diagnosis:

1. Anxiety Disorder - Recurrent sore throat and difficulty swallowing may be symptoms of anxiety disorder due to fear of contracting a serious illness or having a severe infection.
2. Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD) - Frequent upper respiratory infections could indicate GERD, where stomach acid flows back into the esophagus causing irritation and inflammation.
3. Tonsillitis - The presence of enlarged tonsils with exudate suggests a possible case of tonsillitis, which is an infection or inflammation of the tonsils.
4. Cardiomyopathy - A condition where the heart muscle becomes weak and cannot pump blood effectively. It can cause symptoms like fatigue, shortness of breath, and swelling in the legs.
5. Pericarditis - Inflammation of the sac that surrounds the heart. Symptoms include chest pain, fever, and difficulty breathing.
6. Pulmonary Embolism - A blockage in one of the pulmonary arteries, usually caused by a blood clot. It can cause shortness of breath, chest pain, and coughing up blood.
